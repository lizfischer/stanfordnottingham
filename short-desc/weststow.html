West Stow

West Stow is a village in West Suffolk. “West” most likely refers to its westward position in Suffolk county; “Stow” comes from OE stōw, meaning “special place.” In the 1850s, a Saxon cemetery was discovered in the area. However, the site was not further explored until 1947, when Anglo-Saxon huts were uncovered near the site. It was later discovered that the entire area had been covered by sand in the 13th century, which had preserved the medieval village. A reconstruction of the original village is open to visitors.
