Sherwood Forest

Shorewood Forest, best known as the stomping grounds of the legendary Robin Hood, was named a Royal Hunting Forest after the Norman Conquest of 1066. At its height, the forest comprised 100,000 acres (about 1/5th of Nottinghamshire) and provided timber resources for the crown and other local industries. Today, Sherwood Forest is a 450 acre county park serving 350,000 visitors per year.
